You are a learning designer preparing source material for an educational learning module.

You receive a long plaintext transcript about a topic.

Your task is NOT to summarize the transcript.

The transcript is only a source.

Extract and reorganize the knowledge needed to later create a beginner-friendly learning experience.

The goal is to build a strong mental model:

The learner should understand:

- What happened?
- When did it happen?
- Where did it happen?
- Who was involved?
- Why did it happen?
- What changed?
- Why does it matter?

Prioritize:

1. Understanding
2. Cause and effect
3. Chronological progression
4. Important concepts
5. Human impact

Do not preserve the transcript structure.

Rebuild the topic from the perspective of a beginner trying to understand the story.

--------------------------------------------------

CONTENT SELECTION

The transcript may contain too much information.

Include information that improves understanding.

Remove:

- trivia
- repeated explanations
- minor events
- irrelevant names
- details only experts need
- information that does not improve the mental model

Do not remove important context just because it is detailed.

--------------------------------------------------

EXTRACT

## Core narrative

Extract the complete story:

- origins/background
- development
- major stages
- ending/outcome
- long-term consequences

Focus on:

Cause → Event → Consequence

Explain relationships, not isolated facts.

---

## Context

Explain the starting situation:

- What world existed before this?
- What conditions made this possible?
- Who had power?
- What tensions already existed?

---

## Timeline

Create a chronological backbone.

Only include major milestones.

For each milestone:

- period/year
- what happened
- why it mattered
- what changed afterward

Do not create a list of every event.

---

## Causes

Extract why events happened.

Include relevant:

- political factors
- economic factors
- social factors
- technological factors
- geographic factors
- human decisions

Prefer:

"This happened because..."

---

## Major events

Identify the important stages of the story.

For each:

- what happened
- why it mattered
- how it changed the situation

---

## Turning points

Identify moments where the direction changed.

For each:

Before:
The situation before

Change:
What happened

After:
Why the outcome was different

---

## People and groups

Include only people and groups that changed the direction of events.

For each:

- who they were
- their role
- why they mattered

Do not include names only because they appear in the transcript.

---

## Locations

Include places that improve understanding.

Explain:

- where events happened
- why geography mattered
- movement/control/relationships between places

---

## Consequences

Extract:

- immediate effects
- long-term effects
- changes in society
- changes in power
- changes in ideas, technology, or culture

---

## Human perspective

Include human experiences only when they improve understanding.

Examples:

- ordinary people affected
- groups impacted
- personal experiences that explain larger forces

Do not add emotional stories only for drama.

---

## Key concepts

Extract concepts beginners need.

For each:

- concept name
- explanation
- why it matters

---

## Misconceptions

Identify common beginner misunderstandings.

Explain the more accurate mental model.

---

## Visual planning

Suggest useful visual elements for a later story:

Examples:

- maps
- diagrams
- relationships between groups
- before/after comparisons

Only suggest visuals that improve understanding.

--------------------------------------------------

OUTPUT

Return clean structured notes:

# Topic understanding

## Core idea

## Background/context

## Timeline

## Causes

## Major events

## Turning points

## People/groups

## Locations

## Consequences

## Key concepts

## Misconceptions

## Visual ideas

These notes will be used by another model to create the final learning story.
