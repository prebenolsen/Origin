# Meeting People - Section Overview

> Goal: **Make it social** - introduce yourself, say where you're from, what you do,
> who's in your family, what you like and do for fun, and keep a conversation going.

## What you can now do

The first section kept you alive on a trip; this one makes you good company. You can now
*start* a conversation with a stranger and, more importantly, *keep it going* - because
this section drilled the reactions and "ask it back" moves that turn a single answer into
a real chat:

- **Introduce yourself socially** - ask and give names the casual way (`como te llamas` /
  `me llamo`), the all-important "and you?" (`y tu`), "pleased to meet you", "how are
  you?", "how's it going?", and warm goodbyes.
- **Say where you're from and where you live** - and tell the two apart (`soy de` vs
  `vivo en`), ask it back, and say your nationality.
- **Talk about work or study** - ask "what do you do?" and answer with your own job or
  field. (*Personalized* - you drilled the profession that's actually yours.)
- **Talk about family** - say who you have and ask who they have. (*Personalized* - you
  drilled your real family.)
- **Talk about free time and hobbies** - "in my free time I like to...", and the three
  verbs Spanish uses for "play / do": `juego` (sport), `toco` (instrument), `hago`
  (do/make). (*Personalized* - your real hobbies.)
- **Express opinions** - like, love, hate, prefer, don't like; agree ("me too") and
  disagree ("not me"); and the singular vs plural "I like" (`me gusta` / `me gustan`).
- **Ask the social follow-ups** - age, "really?", "how long have you been here?", "how
  nice", "tell me" - the glue that keeps someone talking.
- **Make plans** - "do you fancy...?", "do you want to...?", "shall we meet up?", say
  "okay" (`vale`), or give a soft no.
- **Give and take a compliment, and make small talk** - "you speak Spanish so well!",
  "I like your...", "how cool", "how's everything going?".

## How it was taught - and the pattern you should now notice

Like the first section, this is taught as **chunks** - whole phrases. But the chunks here
quietly start to *rhyme*, and a sharp learner will have begun to feel the pattern:

- **The same verbs keep coming back in the "I" form:** `soy`, `estoy`, `tengo`, `vivo`,
  `trabajo`, `me gusta`, `quiero`. You've now met each of these several times in
  different sentences.
- **The "ask it back" pairs:** `como te llamas` / `me llamo`, `de donde eres` / `soy de`,
  `donde trabajas` / `trabajo en`, `donde vives` / `vivo en`, `tienes` / `tengo`. You can
  see the question and the answer are built from the same parts.
- **Gender has started to surface:** `encantado` / `encantada`, `soy español` /
  `española`, `que guapo` / `que guapa`, and the `-o`/`-a` job endings (`ingeniero` /
  `ingeniera`). The cards told you this happens; the *system* behind it is the next step.

You can now *say* a lot of true things about yourself - but you're still mostly **reciting
memorized lines**. You can't yet freely **swap the pieces** to make a brand-new sentence.
That gap is exactly what the next section is built to close. See
[`../plan-going-forward.md`](../../plan-going-forward.md).

## Every word and phrase taught in this section

Listed by scenario, in teaching order.

### 1. Introductions (social)
| Spanish | English |
|---|---|
| como te llamas | what's your name? |
| me llamo | my name is |
| y tu | and you? |
| encantado | pleased to meet you |
| mucho gusto | nice to meet you |
| igualmente | likewise |
| como estas | how are you? |
| estoy bien | I'm fine |
| que tal | how's it going? |
| me alegro | glad to hear it |
| hasta pronto | see you soon |
| nos vemos | see you around |

### 2. Where you're from
| Spanish | English |
|---|---|
| de donde eres | where are you from? |
| soy de | I'm from |
| vivo en | I live in |
| donde vives | where do you live? |
| el pais | the country |
| soy español | I'm Spanish |

### 3. Work & study *(personalized)*
Core frame taught to everyone:
| Spanish | English |
|---|---|
| a que te dedicas | what do you do? |
| soy | I am (a job) |
| trabajo en | I work at / in |
| estudio | I study |
| donde trabajas | where do you work? |
| una empresa | a company |

Frame drilled: **`Soy ___.`** ("I'm a ___."). You then learned **only the job you
picked**, from these options (each shown with its feminine form):
- **Tech & engineering:** ingeniero/-a (engineer), programador/-a (programmer),
  diseñador/-a (designer)
- **Health:** medico/-a (doctor), enfermero/-a (nurse), dentista (dentist)
- **Education & office:** profesor/-a (teacher), estudiante (student), abogado/-a
  (lawyer), contable (accountant)
- **Trades & service:** cocinero/-a (chef/cook), camarero/-a (waiter), autonomo/-a
  (self-employed)

### 4. Family *(personalized)*
Core frame taught to everyone:
| Spanish | English |
|---|---|
| mi familia | my family |
| tengo | I have |
| tienes | do you have? |
| mi madre | my mother |
| mi padre | my father |
| no tengo | I don't have |

Frame drilled: **`Tengo ___.`** ("I have ___."). You then learned **only the people in
your family**, from these options:
- **Siblings:** un hermano (a brother), una hermana (a sister), dos hermanos (two
  brothers), dos hermanas (two sisters)
- **Children:** un hijo (a son), una hija (a daughter), dos hijos (two children)
- **Partner:** pareja (a partner), novio (boyfriend), novia (girlfriend), marido
  (husband), mujer (wife)

### 5. Hobbies & free time *(personalized)*
Core frame taught to everyone:
| Spanish | English |
|---|---|
| que haces | what do you do? |
| en mi tiempo libre | in my free time |
| me gusta | I like (to) |
| juego | I play (a sport) |
| toco | I play (an instrument) |
| hago | I do / I make |

Frame drilled: **`En mi tiempo libre, me gusta ___.`** ("In my free time, I like to
___."). You then learned **only the activities you picked**, from these options:
- **Sport & exercise:** jugar al futbol (play football), jugar al tenis (play tennis),
  nadar (swim), correr (run), ir al gimnasio (go to the gym)
- **Music & arts:** tocar la guitarra (play the guitar), cantar (sing), bailar (dance),
  pintar (paint)
- **At home:** cocinar (cook), leer (read), ver series (watch series), jugar a
  videojuegos (play video games)
- **Outdoors:** hacer senderismo (go hiking), viajar (travel), hacer fotos (take photos),
  ir a la playa (go to the beach)
