# Visiting Spain - Section Overview

> Goal: **Survive and connect on a trip** - greet people, count and pay, eat out,
> get around, shop, and ask for help. This is the traveller's survival kit.

## What you can now do

By finishing this section you can hold the small, practical exchanges a trip is made
of - without switching to English at the first hurdle:

- **Open and close any interaction politely** - greet by time of day, say please /
  thank you / sorry, and say goodbye.
- **Introduce yourself as a visitor** - say your name, where you're from, that you're
  on holiday, and warn people (kindly) that your Spanish is still small.
- **Handle money and numbers** - ask the price, understand it, and count from 1 to 100.
- **Ask the four questions that unlock everything** - what / who / where / when / why /
  how, plus "where is...?", "at what time...?", "how much...?".
- **Order in a cafe and a restaurant** - drinks, a bite, the menu, still vs sparkling
  water, and ask for the bill.
- **Shop for food and clothes** - find products in a supermarket, ask to try clothes on,
  ask for your size, bigger or smaller. (Both shops are *personalized* - you drilled the
  food and clothes words that are actually yours.)
- **Find your way and take a taxi** - left / right / straight on / near / far, "where is
  the toilet?", and direct a driver to the hotel, airport or station.
- **Tell the days and know if a place is open** - today / tomorrow / yesterday, the seven
  days, open / closed.
- **Rescue a conversation that's going too fast** - "I don't understand", "more slowly
  please", "can you repeat that?", "do you speak English?", "I'm lost".

## How it was taught

Everything here is **useful chunks** - whole phrases you can say as-is (`me llamo`,
`cuanto cuesta`, `donde esta el baño`). You learned to *recognise* and *recall* these,
and the personalized shops let you slot your own words into a frame. You have **not** yet
been taught how the chunks are built or how to swap their pieces - that's the next section.

A note already living in the cards: in Spain the `c`/`z` before `e`/`i` is a soft "th"
(`gracias` = "GRA-thyas", `cerveza` = "ther-VAY-tha"), `ll` sounds like "y", `ñ` like
"ny", and `j` is a throaty "h". You met all four sounds here.

### 7. Supermarket *(personalized)*
Core phrases taught to everyone:
| Spanish | English |
|---|---|
| donde estan | where are |
| cuanto cuesta | how much is it |
| eso es todo | that's all |

Frame drilled: **`Donde estan los/las ___?`** ("Where are the ___?"). You then learned
**only the products you picked**, from these options:
- **Vegetables:** pepino (cucumber), tomates (tomatoes), pimientos (peppers), cebolla
  (onion), patatas (potatoes), lechuga (lettuce), zanahorias (carrots), ajo (garlic)
- **Fruit:** manzanas (apples), platanos (bananas), naranjas (oranges), limones (lemons),
  fresas (strawberries)
- **Food:** pollo (chicken), huevos (eggs), pescado (fish), pan (bread), queso (cheese),
  arroz (rice), pasta (pasta)
- **Drinks:** cafe (coffee), agua con gas (sparkling water), agua sin gas (still water),
  leche (milk), zumo (juice), cerveza (beer), vino (wine)
