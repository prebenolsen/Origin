# Content plan - "Living in Spain" (next chapter)

*A proposed design and table of contents for the second Spanish goal,
`living-in-spain`, which already exists as a stub in `language.json`
(`available: false`, no scenarios). This is a discussion + suggested ToC, not a
full word list - each scenario shows only a handful of example
words/phrases/sentences to set the tone and scope.*

---

## How "Living" should differ from "Visiting"

The "Visiting Spain" goal answers a single question: **can I survive a transaction
and move on?** It is built around one-shot encounters (order, pay, leave). A
resident has a different problem: the *same* people and institutions recur, the
stakes are higher (a contract, a diagnosis, a fine), and the learner needs to
**give information and understand replies**, not just point and pay.

So the design shifts on four axes:

| | Visiting | Living |
|---|---|---|
| **Goal of an exchange** | get the thing, leave | build a relationship / complete a process |
| **Grammar weight** | memorised phrases | first real verbs (present tense), `tu` vs `usted` |
| **Comprehension** | recognise a price | understand an instruction, a form field, a date |
| **Emotional register** | polite stranger | neighbour, patient, tenant, parent, customer-with-a-complaint |

**Design principles to carry over** (they worked): small batches of 3, lead with
the most useful word, personalize where the vocabulary is a matter of personal
circumstance, notes carry the culture. **And fix forward** the issues from the
Visiting review - keep `ñ`, teach `por favor`-style courtesy explicitly, be
consistent about a single form per meaning.

**One new thing Living needs that Visiting could skip:** a light, explicit
introduction to **`tu` vs `usted`** and to a **handful of high-frequency verbs**
(`tener`, `necesitar`, `poder`, `querer`, `haber - hay`). Residents can't get far
on fixed phrases alone; they need to say "*I have* an appointment", "*can you*
send it by email", "*is there* a pharmacy nearby". This should be threaded through
scenarios, not taught as a grammar lecture.

## Suggested table of contents

Ordered roughly by what a new arrival hits first (paperwork and housing) through
to settling in (neighbours, work, complaints). 10 scenarios is a reasonable first
cut; the last two or three can ship later as placeholders.

### 1. `paperwork` - Bureaucracy & the NIE
*The first wall every newcomer hits: appointments, documents, queues.*
- `tengo una cita` - I have an appointment
- `el NIE / el empadronamiento` - the foreigner ID / town-hall registration
- `necesito el documento` - I need the document
- *Example:* `Tengo una cita a las diez para el NIE.` - I have a 10 o'clock
  appointment for the NIE.
- *Teaching note:* introduce `usted` here - you address an official formally.

### 2. `housing` - Renting a flat *(good candidate for personalization)*
*Reading a listing, viewing, signing.*
- `el alquiler / la fianza` - the rent / the deposit
- `el contrato` - the contract
- `los gastos incluidos` - bills included
- *Example:* `Cuanto es el alquiler al mes?` - How much is the rent per month?
- *Personalize:* "what matters to you in a flat?" -> `amueblado` (furnished),
  `ascensor` (lift), `terraza`, `garaje`, `se admiten mascotas` (pets allowed).

### 3. `utilities-internet` - Setting up the home
*Electricity, water, internet, the things you phone a company about.*
- `dar de alta` - to set up / activate (an account)
- `la factura` - the bill/invoice
- `la luz / el agua / internet`
- *Example:* `Quiero dar de alta el internet en esta direccion.`

### 4. `the-doctor` - Health & the pharmacy
*The highest-stakes comprehension scenario; carry over `farmacia` basics from
Visiting and go deeper.*
- `me duele aqui` - it hurts here
- `tengo fiebre / tos` - I have a fever / a cough
- `el centro de salud / la tarjeta sanitaria` - health centre / health card
- *Example:* `Me duele la cabeza desde ayer.` - My head has hurt since yesterday.
- *Teaching note:* introduce `me duele` + body part as a tiny pattern; one batch
  of body parts (`cabeza, estomago, garganta`).

### 5. `the-bank` - Money & accounts
- `abrir una cuenta` - to open an account
- `la tarjeta / el cajero` - the card / the ATM
- `una transferencia` - a transfer
- *Example:* `Quiero abrir una cuenta, por favor.`

### 6. `phone-appointments` - On the phone & booking
*Comprehension-heavy: hold music, "press 1", "next available".*
- `Digame?` - the Spanish way to answer the phone
- `quisiera pedir cita` - I'd like to book an appointment
- `le paso con...` - I'll put you through to...
- *Example:* `Quisiera pedir cita para el lunes por la manana.`
- *Teaching note:* recognise-don't-produce items belong here (you mostly need to
  *understand* the menu).

### 7. `neighbours-smalltalk` - Everyday neighbourhood life
*The relationship layer Visiting never needed.*
- `que tal? / como va todo?` - how's it going?
- `el vecino / la comunidad` - the neighbour / the building residents' assoc.
- `el ascensor esta roto` - the lift is broken
- *Example:* `Buenos dias, que tal? - Bien, y tu?`
- *Teaching note:* the natural place to contrast `tu` (neighbour) with the `usted`
  from scenario 1.

### 8. `work-basics` - At work / job admin *(could defer to Business Spanish)*
- `el horario / la nomina` - the schedule / the payslip
- `tengo una reunion` - I have a meeting
- `puedes enviarme...?` - can you send me...?
- *Note:* keep this thin and everyday; deep professional Spanish is the separate
  `business-spanish` goal.

### 9. `getting-around-living` - Transport as a resident
*Beyond the tourist taxi: passes, recharging, season tickets.*
- `el abono / la tarjeta de transporte` - the travel pass
- `recargar` - to top up
- `que linea va a...?` - which line goes to...?
- *Example:* `Necesito recargar la tarjeta de transporte.`

### 10. `complaints-problems` - When something goes wrong
*Returns, disputes, "this isn't what I ordered/paid for".*
- `hay un problema con...` - there's a problem with...
- `quiero una devolucion` - I want a refund
- `esto no funciona` - this doesn't work
- *Example:* `Hay un problema con la factura de la luz.`

## Cross-cutting suggestions

- **Thread the verbs, don't lecture them.** A short `verbs-you-need` lesson block
  (or notes spread across scenarios 1-3) covering `tener`, `necesitar`, `poder`,
  `querer`, and `hay` (there is/are) will quietly unlock most of the above. Teach
  them as "I have / I need / can I / I want / there is", not as conjugation tables.
- **`tu` vs `usted` is the signature lesson of this goal.** Introduce it in
  `paperwork` (formal) and pay it off in `neighbours-smalltalk` (informal). One
  clear note - "with officials and older strangers use *usted*; with neighbours,
  friends and young people use *tu*" - is enough at this level.
- **Personalize the two scenarios where circumstances vary most:** `housing`
  (flat features) and possibly `the-doctor` (which symptoms/conditions are
  relevant to *you*). Reuse the proven `personalize.json` pattern.
- **Comprehension items.** Living involves understanding replies you didn't script
  (`le paso con`, `digame`, `siguiente`). If the engine supports a
  "recognise only" flag, flag these; if not, mark them clearly in notes so the
  course doesn't drill a learner to *produce* phone-operator phrases they only need
  to *understand*.
- **Apply the Visiting fixes here from day one:** keep `ñ` (`España`, `mañana`,
  `baño`, `contraseña`); teach courtesy words explicitly; one Spanish form per
  English meaning; add 6-9 / tens-pattern numbers since rent, bills and dates all
  demand bigger numbers than a tourist needs.

## Suggested first slice to actually build

If shipping incrementally, the highest-value first three are **`paperwork`,
`housing`, `the-doctor`** - the three things that genuinely block or scare a new
arrival. Build those as `standard`/`personalized`, publish the rest as
`placeholder` scaffolds (English column filled, `es: ""`) so they appear on the
roadmap without being shown to learners, then fill them over time.

When any scenario goes live, remember the repo rule: set the goal's
`available: true` and add the slug to its `scenarios` array in `language.json`,
bump the MINOR version + `package.json`, add a `docs/changelog.md` entry, and mark
the row DONE in `docs/content.md`.
