# Guiding principle going forward

Every future module should be evaluated using one question:

> Does this significantly increase what the learner can express?

High-leverage structural concepts usually do.

Examples:

* and
* but
* because
* also
* very
* present tense
* past tense
* future tense

These unlock thousands of new sentences.

Prioritize lessons that multiply expressive ability before expanding vocabulary into specialized domains.

# Create a Duolingo-style bilingual word matching exercise.

Requirements:
- Display two columns:
  - Left side: words in English
  - Right side: corresponding words in Spanish
- The user clicks one word from either side, then clicks the matching word from the other side to create a pair.
- Correct matches disappear or are marked as completed.
- Incorrect matches give error, and the user can try again

Game flow:
- Only show 6 English–Spanish pairs at a time.
- When all 6 pairs are completed, spawn the next 6 pairs.
- Continue until the full word list has been completed.
- The full word list is generated once at the start and must never change afterward.
- The order of all words must be randomized once when the quiz starts.
- Do not reshuffle, regenerate, or replace remaining pairs during the session.
- The next batch of 6 must come from the already randomized list.

UI:
- Clean, minimal language-learning app style.
- Two vertical columns with clickable word buttons.
- Clearly show selected words.
- Animate completed matches.
- Show progress (for example: “12 / 50 words completed”).
- Make it feel similar to Duolingo’s matching exercises.

Data:
- Use a list of English–Spanish word pairs as the source data.
- Keep the pair relationship intact while randomizing display order.
